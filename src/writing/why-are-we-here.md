I am writing this because if you somehow were to end up on this website, you might wonder what is this, what is the purpose of it, and how do I get somewhere else? I can't help you with the last part at the moment, but I can tell you the rest. I have, like everyone else, seen the rapid development of AI tools pass me by, with me mainly using them to write e-mails and as a more complex Google search.[^1: clarification: This is slightly simplified as I use it on a daily basis for work, but not in a crazy sci-fi capacity]

What I then realized from people close to me, like my own brother and one of my friends I play padel with, is that you can use AI tools (such as Claude Code), to develop websites and applications. Obviously it doesn't do all the work – not yet at least – but that is where I come in, and this website comes in. I don't really code[^2: wry: unless you consider Python coding], and have veered away from the path of a technician as I've heard them be called. But I have always wanted to do a personal project where I learned the process and coding as a whole. There was the one time I started to learn Rust[^3: wry: which quickly reminded me why I dropped out of my Informatics studies. I'm only half-joking there, I mainly forgot about it like most hobby-projects of mine.]

So anyway, I paid for the pro subscription for Claude and got to work. Or more precisely, let Claude Code go to work, while I probably was watching a Let's Play of Terraria by Totalbiscuit and Jesse Cox[^4: wry: highly recommended, and I should probably find a place to recommend YouTube videos now that I think of it…]. This is also only a half-truth, because I still had to create accounts, set up all the website infrastructure and so on. All with the help of Claude and plenty of screenshots copy-pasted to make sure I wasn't doing anything stupid, which I probably was, but I am also fine doing and/or being.

So what is this website again, I hear you asking. Well, it is me just doing something with the use of AI, and the purpose is to change the world for the better! Join me on this journey, because it may or may not be a wild ride, depending on whether I forget about it after my trips to both Berlin[^5: clarification: this month] and Japan[^6: clarification: in June].
